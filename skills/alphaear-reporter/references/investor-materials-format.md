# Investor Materials Format

## When to use this format

Load this file when the output mode is `Investor Materials`.

Use for:
- Investor briefings.
- Board or committee-facing summaries.
- One-page materials.
- Presentation-ready written narrative.
- Stakeholder communication requiring non-technical language.

---

## Key differences from research notes and coverage reports

| Dimension | Research note | Initiating coverage | Investor materials |
|---|---|---|---|
| Length | Short to medium | Long | Short to very short |
| Audience | Internal analysts | Internal / committee | Investors, board, stakeholders |
| Language | Technical acceptable | Technical acceptable | Non-technical required |
| Detail level | High | Very high | Low — summary only |
| Uncertainty language | Explicit | Explicit | Simplified |
| Numbers | Full precision | Full precision | Rounded, contextualized |

---

## Required inputs before writing

| Input | Required |
|---|---|
| Core recommendation | Yes |
| One-line rationale | Yes |
| Key supporting evidence (2–3 points) | Yes |
| Top risk | Yes |
| Valid-as-of date | Yes |
| Audience type | Yes |
| Tone guidance | Recommended |

---

## Format options

### Format A — One-pager

Sections:
1. **What we think** — Recommendation in one sentence.
2. **Why** — Two or three bullet points, each one sentence.
3. **What could go wrong** — One or two bullets.
4. **What we are watching** — One or two monitoring points.
5. **Valid as of** — Date.

Target length: Under 250 words.

### Format B — Executive briefing

Sections:
1. **Situation** — What is happening and why it matters.
2. **Our view** — Recommendation and confidence.
3. **Supporting evidence** — Three points maximum.
4. **Key risk** — One paragraph.
5. **Recommended action** — Specific and time-bound if possible.
6. **Valid as of** — Date.

Target length: 400–600 words.

### Format C — Presentation narrative

Use when the output will be read aloud or converted into slide notes.

Rules:
- Each paragraph maps to one slide or one talking point.
- No paragraph longer than 80 words.
- Lead every paragraph with the conclusion, then support.
- Avoid acronyms without defining them first.
- Remove footnotes and citations from body text; move to an appendix.

---

## Language rules

- No jargon without immediate plain-language translation.
- Replace "significant" with a number or a qualifier that means something.
- Avoid passive voice.
- Do not write "it is important to note that." Just state the point.
- Do not use hedges to avoid taking a position. State the position; then state the uncertainty separately.

## Red Flags to Avoid

- unverifiable claims
- fuzzy market sizing without assumptions
- inconsistent team roles or titles
- revenue math that does not sum cleanly
- inflated certainty where assumptions are fragile

## Quality Gate

Before delivering:
- every number matches the current source of truth
- use of funds and revenue layers sum correctly
- assumptions are visible, not buried
- the story is clear without hype language
- the final asset is defensible in a partner meeting
